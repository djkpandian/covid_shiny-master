March 2, 2020
On Mar. 2, the European Centre for Disease Prevention and Control announced that it has increased the risk level from moderate to high for people in the European Union.
March 3, 2020
On Mar. 3, the World Health Organization announced that the global death rate of the coronavirus was 3.4%, far higher than that of the common flu.
Italy announced that quarantine red zones could be established to contain the spread of the virus.
March 8, 2020
On Mar. 8,over 16 million people were quarantined in Italy.
Schools, gyms, museums, nightclubs and other venues across the country were forced to close aswell.
France banned gatherings of over 1 000 people to stop the spread of the virus.
March 9, 2020
On Mar. 9, Italy imposed a nationwide quarantine. People may only travel for necessity, work and health reasons.
