March 13, 2020
On Mar. 13, Donald Trump officially declared a national emergency, allocating about $50 billion for relief efforts.
The Premier League suspended their season after Arsenal F.C. manager Mikel Arteta and a Chelsea F.C. player tested positive for the disease.
March 17, 2020
On Mar. 17, France imposed a nationwide lockdown.
In order to contain the spread of the coronavirus, citizens in France are generally not allowed to leave their homes for the next 14 days.
The European Union banned non-essential travel from outside the Union to the 26 countries.
The 2020 UEFA European Football Championship has been postponed to the following year.
March 21, 2020
On Mar. 21, Netflix and YouTube reduced their video quality in the European Union to help prevent Internet gridlock as tens of millions of Europeans work at home or self-isolate.
March 22, 2020
On Mar. 22, the federal and state governments of Germany agreed on a "comprehensive ban on contact".
