December 1, 2019
On Dec. 1, the first known patient began to show symptoms.
Others would soon follow.
December 31, 2019
On Dec. 31, the World Health Organization was contacted by the Chinese government and informed of "cases of pneumonia of unknown etiology (unknown cause) detected in Wuhan".
At that time there was no evidence that the virus could be easily spread by humans.
Chinese authorities had identified 266 people who had been infected by the virus by that time.
January 11, 2020
On Jan. 11, Chinese state media reported the first known death caused by the virus.
The man had several serious medical conditions, including chronic liver disease, and died from heart failure and pneumonia.
January 21, 2020
On Jan. 21, the W.H.O. began releasing situation reports on a daily basis.
Cases were reported in Japan, Thailand, South Korea, Taiwan and the United States. The latter beeing the first case outside Asia.
January 23, 2020
On Jan. 23, the city of Wuhan was quarantined from the rest of China.
Planes and trains were not allowed to leave the city, and buses, subways and ferries within the city were suspended.
At this point, over 570 people had been infected and at least 17 were reported dead.
